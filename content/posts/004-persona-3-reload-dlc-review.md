**THIS REVIEW WILL CONTAIN SPOILERS FOR PERSONA 3 RELOAD AND ITS DLC!!!**

*If you are looking for a quick review the first few paragraphs should be
spoiler free and I created a TD;LR section right after the introduction for a
quick spoiler free review.*

Persona is a franchise that I adore. I got introduced to the franchise like
many others did when Joker was added into Smash Bros. Back when I first
heard about it, I was watching a lot of slice of life anime which I think
is the reason why the series clicked so well with me. A game where you take
cool anime stuff, combine it with some awesome combat, all the social sim stuff, 
and on top of that amazing music. My early high school self could not get enough of 
it. I used my Christmas money in 2019 to get a used PS3 with P5 at Gamestop for like $100
and could not stop playing the game until I finished it. Later in 2020, Persona 4 Golden released
on Steam and even though it was older I was able to play through it just fine and really enjoyed it.
As one would I began Persona 3 FES next and could not really get into it. P4G did not feel that
dated compared to P3F so I then tried portable. That didn't really click with me either.
I could write a whole other article about how Persona 3 has a weird problem with the different
versions of the game but I will not get more into that here. Point being is in 2023, Persona 3
Reload was announced, a remake of Persona 3. I was so excited because I was finally going to
have a more modern version of the game that I could enjoy. I did not get the game right away when
it released in February 2024 because I was finishing up playing Trails to Azure but after I beat
it I started it right away. Persona 3 Reload quickly became my favorite game in the franchise
with its darker tone, amazing plot, great characters, and more. 

When the DLC for Persona 3 Reload was announced, I was a bit skeptical. The DLC was targeting
to remake P3F's The Answer epilogue which from what I have heard about from YouTube videos was
not that good to begin with. It also had a ridiculous price tag of $30 and if I recall correctly
was not included in any of the special editions. It launched in September of the same year and to
be honest I just skipped it when it dropped. I was beyond locked in playing the Trails series at
that point and I'm pretty sure I was playing Cold Steel IV which is the climax of the Erebonia arc.
When I played P3R, I did end up platinum (getting all the achievements) on my first playthrough and
the DLC took the platinum away so I knew I would play it eventually. During the Steam 2025 winter
sale, I finally decided to pick the DLC up due to it being on sale for $10 and told myself I would
play it after I beat Trails beyond the Horizon which was coming out on January 15th of 2026. So after
beating that I finally got to playing the DLC and I went in expecting it to be not so great. Today,
I beat the game so I want to write my review of it.

That was a bit of a long background dump haha. 
The review will be divided into four sections. TD;LR, for the people who want a quick spoiler free
answer. The Good, where I talk about the things that I like, The Bad, where I talk about the many
things I did not like, and then my Final Verdict where I talk about if I think the game is worth
getting.

## TD;LR

TD;LR, I did not really like Episode Aigis. There was little to no plot the entire way through,
it was mostly just more tartarus with almost nothing new, and it really just felt like a slog to
get through. If you enjoyed Persona 3 Reload and want to see a little bit more of the main cast
after the ending of the main game then I would play it. If you're satisfied with the ending of
the game then just skip this. 

Score: 5/10

## The Good

First up, what I thought was good. First off the new battle theme and opening are really good tracks.
It's Persona music though so you kind of expected it to be good but even the tracks that were in the
original that were remade like Heartful Cry also in my opinion have their best versions here.

Next, the combat and building Personas is still fun (for the most part more on that later). There is
just something to comboing strings together with hitting weaknesses that is really fun. I also really
love building sets in RPGs and fusing Personas has always been a super fun mechanic. I also thought
the Joker boss fight that was there is a really neat addition even if Persona 5 is milked to death.
Having little boss fights that tie into the other games is things that I love to see. Like how you
can fight the P3 and P4 protagonists in Persona 5 Royal. I did skip this boss just cause I wanted to
be done with this but maybe one day I'll go back and do it. You also can buy back Personas you had
in the compendium from your main save which I love. You have to be on Hard or lower for this and I
played on Heartless for most of the game so I didn't get to use it all too much but it's neat that
you can do that.

Visually, the game is still stunning. All the new animations here look really good and even after two
years the style is still just top notch in this game.

Now for the parts of the plot that I liked. I liked the conflict towards the end of the game. It was
an interesting question that they were trying to answer and not having the party agree on something and
having tension be super high was cool. I did not really like how the conflict was resolved but more on
that in the next section. I also liked the closure all the main party members got at the end. From what
I remember of the last few days of Persona 3 the other party members are just kind of there and have
a little dialogue talking about how you don't look so good right before you die so having them have
some nice moments here and actually get to see how they react and eventually feel about the main
character dying was good. It also sets up their futures pretty well, it's clear they're now setting out
to try and help humanity as a whole so they no longer want to collectively bring back Nyx.

Also the game has one of the coolest boss fights I've ever seen. You fight a distorted version of the
main character due to him being the root of why the main party is experiencing this and during the
fight he uses the Personas of the party members you have with you and they all have little dialogue
for when he does. Super super cool in my opinion, I just wish they selected a different battle theme.
If it played like a distorted version of Burn My Dread -Last Battle- I would have been flipping out.

That really wraps up all of the good I had to say about this now on to the bad.

## The Bad

So if you read the TD;LR section you know I ranked this game a 5/10. Above seemed pretty positive
but to me there were a lot more things that frustrated me.

First, I just want to talk about the general structure of the DLC. It was basically 150+ more floors
of tartarus with a cutscene looking into the past after 15-25 floors. These cutscenes were of the
moments that the main party awakened to their Personas. Cool to see but they really did not add
anything to the plot. It was really just we need to get down there and see what's there sort of deal.
It really just felt like you were grinding through all of the floors for just a little bit of story
that was pretty ok. There was almost nothing new with the dungeon crawling here so it was just a slog.
The background for the areas weren't even distinct. I swear it just rotated between the same three
themes every 10 or so floors and that was that. At least in the main game, the tartarus backdrops
kept getting weirder and weirder as you progressed but here no. There was also almost no other content
besides the dungeon crawling. There were little social events with the main party you could do in the
dorm after you collected items. These basically were nothing. They unlocked the little buffs your
party members get but in the end it really was nothing and you could do that at any time after getting
the item so it wasn't like it was built into the schedule like into the main game. I wish there were
other things here where you could see how the other characters that were present in social links
reacted to the main character's death.

The DLC felt super grindy and this I feel like was for two reasons. First reason, basically what I 
mentioned above you're just grinding floors the whole game. Second reason, there were no XP bonuses
when fusing Personas. Your social link bonuses should have carried over or something because this
just made it feel like you could not build Personas past their innate skill list. I ended up just
sticking to one really strong physical AOE Persona the whole game and that worked up until the end
game where the difficulty spiked super crazy for no reason.

Next point I didn't like was the difficulty spike at the end. After you clear the floors, there is
three boss fights where you fight the main party in pairs of two. I do not know what they were
expecting here because in the previous boss fight I was taking like no damage at all and doing fine
then in these fights I was getting one shot constantly. I did not want to grind and try to build good
Personas because of the lack of bonus XP so I just set the difficulty down to normal and bought
back my 99 strength physical monster Siegfried from the base game and just went crazy. Throughout the
whole time playing I also got super frustrated with the party leader dies its GG. Throughout the game,
I probably lost like 5 hours total to just random BS from this, so that was fun. 

Now for plot stuff I wasn't a fan of. Like I said in the good I liked the conflict at the end of the
game. The party members were decided if they should go back into the past and save the protagonist
from dying but they'd have to do it all again or just continue in the future like normal. Some wanted
to go back and some didn't. Very interesting conflict however, the way it was decided was they all just
fought. Everyone was given a piece of a key. and all of them had to agree to make the full key or this
arena place would trap the person if they lost the fight and give the winner the key piece. After the
fights too they would just say a few lines of dialogue and then disappear there really wasn't much
resolution between the party they all just kind of agreed after everything else they saw. It would
have been so much better if they learned what they did like mid way through and the rest of the game
was them figuring things out as they continued. The vibes between the main party would have been 10x
different during this and maybe would have made things more interesting.

## Final Verdict

That really sums up my thoughts on Persona 3 Reload's Episode Aigis. It wasn't terrible but it wasn't
great either. Like I said in the TD;LR, my final score is a 5/10. It really only took me around 30 hours
to play this so it really was not long. It really didn't feel like 30 hours either, which is so strange,
I guess that more tartarus floors padding just does that. If you did not enjoy tartarus then you for
sure would not like this but if you liked the plot I would maybe just watch the like ~2 hours of
cutscenes on YouTube or something.

At least I got my platinum back so all is good in the world!
