Hello, everyone!

This is my first post here on this new blog I'm starting up. This is actually my third time attempting to blog haha.
The first time was back in high school during my senior year when I was first learning to code. I wanted to just yap about 
all the cool stuff I was taking in and trying to learn. The second time was during my junior year of college where I wanted 
to do the same thing, but with the intent of having a breadcrumb trail of my work that I was doing in my personal time.
This third time, I just wanted to have a space where I can talk about the various things that I enjoy in life like all sorts of 
tech, games, skiing, and other stuff I do. I think the reason those other two blogs failed was because I was taking it too seriously.
This time around I'm just going to write a bit and just dump my thoughts into this.This blog is hosted on my homelab, so if 
it ever goes down then you can still read the articles on the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/cjcocokrisp/blog) for the blog.
I'll write a post at some point about my homelab and how this blog is actually run because I personally think the setup used some really good tech.

Anyway, enough with the preface.
I'm Christopher. I recently graduated from the University of Massachusetts Lowell with a BA in Computer Science. I'm an Associate Software 
Engineer at Red Hat working on OpenShift GitOps, so for those who know about the Kubernetes ecosystem our upstream is Argo CD.
I started programming back during my sophomore year of high school because I wanted to make stupid little projects like Discord bots and games. 
My first language was Python, but I also learned a bit about Java at the time because I was doing FRC robotics which used Java and C++.
When I was younger, I liked to tinker with modding software for Nintendo consoles which is where I got that spark for programming.
My main interests in technology are cloud native technologies, distributed systems, and networking. I am also trying to learn more about 
electronics right now due to wanting to make some cool physical products.
Beyond technology, I really like playing video games. My favorite genres are fighting games and RPGs.
If I had to list my top 5 games they would be:

1) Trails into Reverie  
2) NieR Automata  
3) Super Smash Bros. Ultimate  
4) Pokemon White Version 2  
5) Risk of Rain 2  

I would put all Trails games on that list (it's my favorite franchise and probably piece of media ever haha) but I can't do that.
I want to try and write my thoughts about the gaming industry and also the games I'm playing here as well.
Some other hobbies I have are skiing which is something that I'm trying to do more in the winter now that I have graduated.

So yeah, I think that's really what I want to talk about for this introduction post.
I don't know how long my articles are going to be but I do have a note on my phone of ideas for a bunch that I want to write.
I also want to expand this at some point and add other stuff like likes, comments, and maybe even a subscribe feature, but we'll see.
You also might be wondering what the `[001]` in the title is. That's just the number of the article this is. I don't know why but I did this with all my blogs so I intend to do that for this too.

That's all for this post, see you all in the next one!
